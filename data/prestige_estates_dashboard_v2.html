CH4.SDF.1 Critical

No Data Protection Officer Appointed

Prestige Estates almost certainly qualifies as an SDF (1,000+ employees, large-scale sensitive data including Aadhaar and PAN). Section 10(2)(a) mandates a DPO based in India. Without a DPO, the four SDF obligations cannot be systematically met and Board proceedings can be initiated suo motu.

CM.GRANULAR.2 Critical

Consent Bundling — Service Access Tied to Marketing

Property enquiry and booking flows appear to bundle service-essential processing with marketing consent. Section 6(1) explicitly prohibits conditioning service access on consent that is not necessary for the service. This is a potential dark-pattern violation — the highest-scrutiny category for regulators.

CH2.SECURITY.1 & CH2.SECURITY.2 Critical

CRM Permission Leaks & No Least-Privilege Access

Pre-sales, sales, and marketing teams have overlapping, over-broad access to customer PII in the internal CRM. Permission leaks mean unauthorized personnel can view Aadhaar numbers, PAN, banking details, and financial records. Section 8(4) requires reasonable safeguards — this gap is a direct violation.

CH2.CONSENT.3 Critical

Consent Withdrawal Requires Email or Phone Call

Section 6(6) mandates withdrawal be "as easy as giving consent." Consent is given via online form or checkbox. Withdrawal requires contacting the company by email or phone. This asymmetry is a direct Section 6(6) breach and likely to be one of the first enforcement priorities for the Data Protection Board.

CH4.CHILD.1 & CH4.CHILD.2 Critical

No Children's Data Protections — Zero Documentation

Section 9 prohibitions on tracking and behavioral monitoring of under-18 users are absolute. Prestige Estates' website analytics, digital marketing, and lead nurturing pipelines have no child suppression logic. Neither restriction is documented in policy. Children's data is the highest-scrutiny category under DPDPA.

Consent Not Demonstrably Free, Specific & Unconditional

Checkboxes and forms exist online, but physical collection points (site visits, head office) use ad-hoc paper forms with no standardised, purpose-specific consent capture. Section 6(1) requires consent to be free, specific, informed, unconditional, and with a clear affirmative action at every collection point.

Privacy policy applies parental consent requirements to children under 13, but DPDPA Section 9(1) requires verifiable parental consent for all persons under 18. The policy is materially incorrect. No age verification exists at any digital touchpoint to identify and protect minor users.

( 06 )   Remediation Roadmap
5 Strategic Initiatives
5
Non-compliant requirements have been clustered by root cause into five named programs. Execute in priority order — Initiatives 1–3 run in parallel from Day 1. Initiative 5 (people) unblocks policy work across all clusters.
Process INIT-001  ·  Priority 1

Consent Management Platform Implementation

Redesign all consent capture flows (digital and physical) to be purpose-specific, granular, and withdrawable with equal ease. Centralise consent records across CRM and offline channels. Implement consent withdrawal dashboard. Addresses the single largest cluster of non-compliant requirements.
